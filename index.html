Fundamental Theorem of Calculus


Part I (Differentiation of the Integral)
Let \(f:[a,b] \to \mathbb{R}\) be continuous and define \[ F(x)=\int_a^x f(t)\,dt. \] Then \[ F'(x)=f(x), \quad x\in(a,b). \]
Part II (Evaluation of Definite Integrals)
If \(F\) is any antiderivative of \(f\) on \([a,b]\) (i.e., \(F'(x)=f(x)\)), then \[ \int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b)-F(a). \]
